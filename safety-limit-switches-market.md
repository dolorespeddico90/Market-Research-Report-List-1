<p><p>Safety limit switches in the market can be categorized by action type into two main segments: mechanical action and non-mechanical action. Mechanical action switches rely on physical movement to operate, ensuring a direct response to conditions. Non-mechanical action switches utilize various sensing technologies, such as magnetic or proximity sensors, to detect the position of an object. Each type serves distinct applications, with mechanical switches being favored for traditional machinery, while non-mechanical types are increasingly used in automated and modern systems for enhanced reliability and safety.</p></p>
