<p><p>The superconductor market is categorized into two main types: Low-Temperature Superconductors (LTS) and High-Temperature Superconductors (HTS). LTS, typically operating below 30 Kelvin, are commonly utilized in MRI machines and particle accelerators, known for their reliability and established technology. In contrast, HTS, functioning above 77 Kelvin, are emerging in applications like power transmission and magnetic levitation due to their higher critical temperatures and potential for efficiency. Both markets are expanding, driven by advancements in materials and growing demand for energy-efficient technologies.</p></p>
