<p><p>The Pipeline Embolization Device (PED) is a specialized endovascular device designed for the treatment of intracranial aneurysms. It functions by diverting blood flow away from the aneurysm, promoting thrombosis and thereby reducing the risk of rupture. The PED is primarily employed in neurovascular procedures and is valued for its ability to treat complex aneurysms that may not be amenable to traditional surgical techniques.</p><p>The PED market is witnessing significant growth, driven by an increase in the prevalence of intracranial aneurysms, advancements in technology, and rising awareness of minimally invasive procedures. The aging population and the growing incidence of stroke and other vascular diseases further contribute to market expansion. Additionally, the integration of new materials and innovative designs is enhancing device performance and patient outcomes.</p><p>Latest trends in the PED market include the development of bio-resorbable stents and continued research aimed at improving safety and efficacy profiles.
