<p><p>The Audio Bone Conduction Sensors market comprises two primary types: piezoelectric and electromagnetic. Piezoelectric sensors utilize materials that generate an electrical charge in response to mechanical stress, enabling efficient sound transmission through bones. In contrast, electromagnetic sensors operate by converting electrical energy into mechanical vibrations using magnetic fields, which also facilitate sound conduction through the skull. Both types harness the body's natural ability to transmit sound via bone, catering to diverse applications in hearing devices, telecommunications, and audio technology.</p></p>
