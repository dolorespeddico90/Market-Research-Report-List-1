<p><p>The Digital Fault Recorder (DFR) market is segmented by sampling frequency into three types: less than 10kHz, 10kHz to 20kHz, and more than 20kHz. DFRs with less than 10kHz are cost-effective and suitable for basic fault detection in low-voltage systems. The 10kHz to 20kHz range offers higher precision, ideal for medium-voltage networks and detailed transient analysis. DFRs with over 20kHz provide ultra-high resolution, catering to critical high-voltage systems and complex fault analysis, ensuring accurate capture of fast transients and harmonics.</p></p>
