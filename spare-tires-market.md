<p><p>The spare tires market includes two main types: inner tube and tubeless. Inner tube tires have a separate rubber tube inside the tire that holds air, offering simplicity and ease of repair but are less common today. Tubeless tires, more prevalent in modern vehicles, rely on an airtight seal between the tire and rim, providing better performance, reduced puncture risks, and improved fuel efficiency. Both types cater to different vehicle needs, with tubeless dominating due to technological advancements and convenience.</p></p>
